# Kubikart Business Master Document

Last updated: June 2026
Business name: **Kubikart**
Location focus: **Ulm / Neu-Ulm / Augsburg region, Germany**
Business type: personalised products, laser cutting/engraving, 3D printing, vinyl cutting, small business signage, and future Werbetechnik services.

---

# 1. Business Summary

Kubikart is a small creative manufacturing business focused on **personalised gifts, custom signage, small business branding products, and local B2B visibility solutions**.

The business combines:

- 3D printing
- laser cutting and engraving
- vinyl cutting
- Epson-based small-format printing
- lamination
- NFC/QR products
- future sublimation
- future Werbetechnik/signage production

The goal is to build a practical, low-risk local and online business that can first generate **€600–€1,000/month profit**, then grow into a more complete Werbetechnik and personalised production studio.

The business should not initially focus on complex services like car wrapping, full outdoor facade signs, or large illuminated signs. Instead, the first focus should be **small, repeatable, profitable products** that can be produced with current or near-future equipment.

# 8. Core Production Workflows

## Workflow 1: Cut Vinyl Lettering

Used for:

- window stickers
- door stickers
- opening hours
- frosted glass
- PVC signs
- acrylic signs
- mirror stickers

Process:

1. Create vector design.
2. Load coloured adhesive vinyl into plotter.
3. Plotter cuts the design.
4. Weed the vinyl by removing unwanted parts.
5. Apply transfer tape over the remaining design.
6. Peel backing paper away.
7. Position design on final surface.
8. Squeegee firmly.
9. Remove transfer tape.
10. Final vinyl remains on glass/acrylic/PVC/wood.

Important:

Transfer tape is temporary. It keeps all letters and logo parts aligned while moving the design from the backing paper to the final surface.

---

## Workflow 2: Epson Print + Laminate + Cut

Used for:

- sticker sheets
- logo stickers
- QR stickers
- labels
- small product decals
- packaging stickers

Process:

1. Design sticker sheet in A3.
2. Print on vinyl sticker paper using Epson ET-8550.
3. Let ink dry properly.
4. Laminate with A3 laminator.
5. Use plotter registration/OPOS/contour-cut feature if available.
6. Weed/remove excess.
7. Package as sticker sheet or individual stickers.

Limitations:

Best for indoor/light-use products. Not for long-term outdoor professional signage.

---

## Workflow 3: Laser Cut / Engrave

Used for:

- wood signs
- photo frames
- QR stands
- name signs
- keychains
- acrylic/wood product parts
- small business signs
- gift products

Process:

1. Create vector design.
2. Prepare material.
3. Mask material if needed.
4. Cut or engrave with laser.
5. Clean edges/smoke marks.
6. Assemble with printed, vinyl, or 3D printed parts.
7. Photograph and package.

Important:

The current diode laser is not ideal for clear acrylic. Use wood, black acrylic, dual-colour acrylic, or outsource/upgrade later for clear acrylic.

---

## Workflow 4: 3D Print Support Parts

Used for:

- bases
- stands
- holders
- custom brackets
- product display props
- letter keychains
- NFC holders
- QR stand supports

Process:

1. Design in Fusion 360, Blender, OpenSCAD, or similar.
2. Print prototype.
3. Test fit with acrylic/wood/PVC parts.
4. Improve model.
5. Print final version.
6. Combine with laser/vinyl/printed parts.

---

## Workflow 5: Sublimation Future Workflow

Used for:

- coated MDF
- coated acrylic blanks
- mugs
- photo panels
- wedding/family gifts

Process:

1. Create design.
2. Print mirror image using sublimation printer and sublimation ink.
3. Place print on sublimation blank.
4. Use heat press with correct time, pressure, and temperature.
5. Remove paper.
6. Let cool.
7. Package.

Important:

Only use sublimation-ready coated blanks unless using proper coatings.

---

## Workflow 6: Outsourced Large-Format Print

Used for:

- large outdoor signs
- large printed vinyl
- professional full-colour signs
- large menu boards
- large posters
- full-colour window graphics

Process:

1. Design artwork.
2. Prepare correct print file.
3. Send to large-format print partner.
4. Receive printed/laminated material.
5. Mount to board or deliver/install if simple.
6. Add markup for design, handling, installation, and customer service.

This avoids buying a large printer too early.

# 18. Core Business Philosophy

Kubikart should grow step by step.

The business should not start by copying a large Werbetechnik company with €100k+ machines. Instead, it should start with small, profitable, repeatable products that solve real problems for local businesses and personal gift customers.

The first goal is not to own every machine.

The first goal is to get customers, prove demand, and create reliable product systems.

Best early positioning:

**Kubikart creates personalised gifts and small business branding products using laser cutting, 3D printing, vinyl cutting, printing, and custom design.**

Best local B2B message:

**We help small businesses in Ulm/Neu-Ulm look more professional with QR signs, window stickers, menus, price boards, and custom signage.**
